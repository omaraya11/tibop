Link to google map

    See traffic or search for places for your drive

    Note: This feature is only available on Android devices and in some countries. Using Google Maps, you can see traffic for your drive, search for place easily, or navigate to a place quickly even if you don't enter a destination in Maps.

    See traffic for your drive

    To see traffic for your drive, follow the steps below.
  1. click on Link to google map.
  2. Touch the Menu .
  3. Choose Start driving.
    4. You'll also see: How long it takes to drive to a suggested destination. Destinations are shown based on data from Google Account settings. Traffic delays, like accidents and construction work, on the way.

    Create a driving shortcut

    To see traffic easily for your drive, create a driving shortcut for your mobile screen:
  1. click on Link to google map.
  2. In the top left, touch the Menu .
  3. Choose Settings > Navigation settings > Add Driving shortcut.

    Get driving notifications using Bluetooth

    If you have Bluetooth turned on and your phone or tablet is paired to your car, you'll get a driving notification when you start your car. To turn on notifications, follow the steps below.
  1. click on Link to google map.
  2. In the top left, touch the Menu .
  3. Choose Settings > Navigation settings > Driving notifications.
  4. Search and navigate to a place.
    To search for and navigate to a place for your drive
    follow the steps below.
  1. click on Link to google map.
  2. In the top left, touch the Menu .
  3. Choose Start driving.
  4. In the top right, touch Search .
  5. Touch a category or search for a specific place.
  6. You'll see places near your route as red dots. To see a place's name and how long it takes to get there, touch a dot.
  7. To go to that place, touch Navigate. If you don't want to go there, touch Dismiss. If you don't choose an option, you'll automatically navigate to that place.
    Tips:
  • You can navigate to a place by touching the "Set Destination" box at the top and entering a place.
  • You can see alternate routes when you start navigation.
